## 1. PURPOSE & SCOPE

This KYC / AML Program describes the policies, procedures, and controls implemented by **[COMPANY NAME]** to:

- Prevent the use of its products and services for money laundering, terrorist financing, sanctions evasion, fraud, or other financial crime.
- Comply with applicable laws and regulations in the jurisdictions where it operates, including (as applicable) anti–money laundering (AML), counter–terrorist financing (CTF), and sanctions regimes.

This Program applies to:

- All products and services offered by [COMPANY NAME]
- All legal entities within the [COMPANY GROUP] that provide financial services or related infrastructure
- All employees, officers, contractors, and relevant third parties

## 2. RISK-BASED APPROACH

[COMPANY NAME] uses a **risk-based approach (RBA)** consistent with global standards (e.g., FATF Recommendations):

- Identify inherent risks (customer types, products, geographies, delivery channels)
- Assess those risks (low / medium / high)
- Apply **controls and enhanced measures** proportionate to those risks

### 2.1 Risk Factors Considered

- **Customer Risk:** nature of business, ownership structure, PEP status, reputation
- **Product / Service Risk:** stablecoins, RWAs, OTC trading, custodial wallets, etc.
- **Geographic Risk:** country risk ratings (sanctions, high-risk/monitored jurisdictions)
- **Channel Risk:** non-face-to-face onboarding, intermediaries, agents, etc.

### 2.2 Risk Classification

Customers and relationships are classified into risk tiers (e.g. Low / Medium / High) and subject to corresponding:

- KYC documentation requirements
- Level of due diligence (CDD vs EDD)
- Monitoring intensity and review frequency

## 3. CUSTOMER DUE DILIGENCE (CDD)

[COMPANY NAME] implements **Customer Due Diligence (CDD)** prior to entering into a business relationship or executing specified transactions.

### 3.1 When CDD is Required

- Establishing a new customer relationship
- Material changes in relationship or ownership
- Transactions above defined thresholds
- Suspicion of money laundering or terrorist financing
- Doubts about the veracity of previously obtained information

### 3.2 CDD for Natural Persons

Minimum information:

- Full legal name
- Date of birth
- Residential address
- Nationality
- Government-issued ID number and document (passport, ID card, etc.)
- Purpose and intended nature of the relationship
- Source of funds / source of wealth (as appropriate to risk)

Verification using:

- Government-issued photo ID
- Independent, reliable sources (e.g., databases, credit bureaus, identity providers)
- Sanctions and PEP screening systems

### 3.3 CDD for Legal Entities

Minimum information:

- Legal name and trading name (if any)
- Legal form and registration number
- Registered and principal business address
- Country of incorporation and operation
- Nature of business and purpose of relationship
- Identity of directors / senior management
- **Beneficial owners** (UBOs) – natural persons with ≥ [X%] ownership or control
- Source of funds and wealth (as appropriate to risk)

Verification using:

- Company registry extracts
- Incorporation documents and corporate resolutions
- Ownership / shareholder registers
- Independent databases

## 4. ENHANCED DUE DILIGENCE (EDD)

Additional measures are applied to **higher-risk customers**, including but not limited to:

- Politically exposed persons (PEPs) and their associates
- Customers in high-risk jurisdictions
- Complex ownership structures with no clear economic rationale
- High-value OTC or large-volume crypto/fiat flows

EDD measures may include:

- Additional identification documents and corroborating evidence
- Detailed source of wealth and source of funds questionnaires
- Adverse media checks and enhanced screening
- Senior management approval before onboarding
- Tighter transaction limits and ongoing monitoring

---

## 5. SANCTIONS & RESTRICTED JURISDICTIONS

[COMPANY NAME] maintains a robust **sanctions screening** and **country-risk framework**.

### 5.1 Sanctions Lists

At a minimum, customers and transactions are screened against:

- UN sanctions lists
- OFAC (U.S.)
- EU consolidated list
- UK HMT list
- Other national or regional lists as applicable

### 5.2 Restricted / Prohibited Countries

[COMPANY NAME]:

- **Prohibits** onboarding or transacting with persons located in or associated with **prohibited jurisdictions** (list maintained in an internal appendix).
- May apply **enhanced controls** or limitations for customers from **high-risk or monitored jurisdictions**.

Screening is performed at:

- Onboarding (KYC)
- Periodic refresh
- When new information arises (e.g., sanctions updates)

## 6. ONGOING MONITORING

KYC is not "one and done." [COMPANY NAME] performs **ongoing monitoring** of customers and activity.

### 6.1 Transaction Monitoring

- Monitoring systems and/or manual reviews are used to detect:
  - Unusual patterns or sudden changes in behavior
  - Structuring / smurfing behavior around thresholds
  - Flows involving high-risk jurisdictions or entities
  - Activity inconsistent with the customer's profile

- Alerts are triaged, investigated, and documented.
- Confirmed suspicious activity is escalated to Compliance.

### 6.2 Periodic KYC Reviews

Risk-based refresh cycles:

- High-risk customers: **at least annually**
- Medium-risk: **every 2–3 years**
- Low-risk: **every 3–5 years** or on trigger events

Trigger events include:

- Change of ownership or control
- Significant increase in volume or change in behavior
- Negative news / adverse media
- Regulatory or law-enforcement inquiries

## 7. SUSPICIOUS ACTIVITY REPORTING (SAR / STR)

[COMPANY NAME] will:

- Identify and investigate suspicious activity or transactions.
- Where required by law, file **Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs / STRs)** to the relevant Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU) or authority in the relevant jurisdiction.
- Maintain confidentiality and ensure that no "tipping off" occurs.

The process includes:

1. Internal detection or staff escalation
2. Review and analysis by Compliance
3. Decision to file or not file a report
4. Documentation of rationale and supporting evidence
5. Filing within statutory timelines

[COMPANY NAME] maintains records in line with applicable laws and regulations, including:

- Customer identification and verification data
- Corporate documents and beneficial ownership information
- Transaction records and account activity
- SAR/STR filings and internal investigation logs
- KYC / EDD files and risk assessments

Records are:

- Retained for at least **[X years]** after the end of the relationship or as required by law
- Stored securely with appropriate access controls
- Available to competent authorities upon lawful request

## 9. GOVERNANCE, ROLES & RESPONSIBILITIES

### 9.1 Board & Senior Management

The Board (or equivalent governing body) is ultimately responsible for:

- Approving the KYC / AML Program
- Ensuring adequate resources for compliance
- Overseeing the effectiveness of AML/CTF controls

### 9.2 Compliance Function

[COMPANY NAME] designates a **Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO)** or equivalent, responsible for:

- Implementation and maintenance of the Program
- Review of alerts and decisions on SAR/STR filings
- Acting as primary liaison with regulators and law-enforcement
- Reporting periodically to senior management and/or the Board

### 9.3 All Staff

All employees have a duty to:

- Follow the policies and procedures in this Program
- Complete AML training as required
- Escalate unusual or suspicious activity promptly
